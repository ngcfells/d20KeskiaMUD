Initiative: +0

Armor Class: 15 (+1 size, +2 natural, +2 heavy wooden shield), touch 11, flat-footed 15

Full Attack: Stalactite (dagger) +2 melee (1d3/19-20)
Space/Reach: 5 ft./5 ft.

Special Qualities: Low-light vision, plant traits, light sensitivity
Saves: Fort +4, Ref +0, Will +0

Combat
Fungus men are cautious, preferring to hide in the damp shadows of their caves. They use improvised stalactite daggers and "shields" harvested from giant mushroom tops.
Spore Cloud (Ex): Once per day as a standard action, a fungus man can emit an opaque cloud of spores in a 15-foot diameter centered on itself. This cloud provides total concealment (50% miss chance) to all creatures within it and lasts for 1d4 rounds. Fungus men typically use this to cover their retreat.
Light Sensitivity (Ex): Fungus men are dazzled in bright sunlight or within the radius of a daylight spell.
Skills: *Fungus men receive a +4 racial bonus on Hide checks when in marshy or cavernous terrain with significant fungal growth.

Camouflage (Ex): Since a Living Hill looks like a normal grassy hill when at rest, it takes a DC 30 Spot check to notice it before it attacks. Anyone camping on the hill is considered to be within the creature's "space."
Silent Swallow (Ex): When the Living Hill feeds on a sleeping or unconscious victim, it opens a sod-lined maw beneath them. The victim must succeed on a DC 29 Reflex save or be swallowed. Because the process is nearly soundless, other sleeping creatures must succeed on a DC 25 Listen check to wake up. Even if awake, companions have only a 1-in-6 chance (roll 1d6, result of 6) to notice the victim's sudden absence unless they are observing them directly.
Swallowed Whole (Ex): A swallowed creature is considered incapacitated by paralytic gastric enzymes (DC 27 Fortitude save or paralyzed). The stomach deals 2d6+12 points of bludgeoning damage and 2d6 points of acid damage per round. If the victim remains inside for 10 rounds, they are completely dissolved. A swallowed creature can cut its way out by using a light slashing or piercing weapon to deal 30 points of damage to the gizzard (AC 14).
Skills: *A Living Hill gains a +20 racial bonus on Hide checks in wilderness settings due to its natural appearance.

Breath Weapon (Su): 30-ft. cone of fuming formic acid. Initial damage 6d6 acid plus permanent blindness; Reflex DC 32 for half damage and blindness for 1 round only. On a failed save, the victim takes an additional 6d6 acid damage every round until the acid is neutralized (requiring a full-round action and at least a gallon of water or a Purify Food and Drink spell).
Crushing Pin (Ex): On a natural attack roll of 16–20 with a claw, the Anthrosaur automatically initiates a grapple. If successful, the victim is pinned. To escape, the victim must succeed on a Grapple check or an Escape Artist check. In place of the standard check, the victim may attempt a "Desperation Strength Check" (1d100); they escape if they roll equal to or under (Victim HD / 27) x 100.
Frightful Presence (Ex): When the Anthrosaur attacks or roars, it inspires terror in all creatures within 60 feet that have fewer HD than it. Affected creatures must succeed on a DC 25 Will save or become shaken for 4d6 rounds. A successful save grants immunity to this Anthrosaur's presence for 24 hours.
Sever (Ex): If the Anthrosaur’s bite confirms a critical hit, the target must succeed on a DC 38 Fortitude save or be bitten in half, resulting in instant death.
Knockback (Ex): Any creature of Large size or smaller hit by the tail slap is thrown 3d10 feet away and takes 1d6 Dexterity damage from a broken bone.
Brittle-Foe Hide (Ex): Any non-magical weapon that strikes the Anthrosaur has a 50% chance of shattering instantly. Magic weapons reduce this chance by 5% per point of enhancement bonus (e.g., a +5 longsword has a 25% chance of breaking).
Damage Reduction (Ex): 15/Epic.

Acidic Bite (Ex): A termite person’s bite deals 1 point of piercing damage plus 1d4 points of acid damage.
Fungicidal Fury (Ex): When a termite person sees a creature with the Plant type (specifically Myconids or Fungus Men), it enters a berserk state. It gains a +1 morale bonus on attack rolls and damage rolls, but takes a –2 penalty to Armor Class. This fury lasts until the fungal creature is dead or out of sight.

If a termite person colony is reduced to 50% of its original numbers in a single encounter, all remaining termite people must succeed on a DC 15 Will save or become shaken and attempt to flee.
Tremorsense (Ex): Termite people can detect and pinpoint the location of anything in contact with the ground within 30 feet.

The Coerl’s Psionic-Tech Battery
These weapons do not use traditional power cells. Instead, they are fueled by the Coerl’s own internal energy—specifically the phosphorus it consumes.
Psionic Recharge (Ex):
A Coerl’s technological weapon has a "buffer" of 10 charges.
Consumption: Each shot consumes 1 charge.

Looting Note (For Players)
If a player manages to scavenge one of these weapons, it is Depleted.

Psionic Users: A character with a power point reserve can "prime" the weapon by spending 3 Power Points as a standard action. This provides enough energy for one shot.
Use Technological Device: To understand this interface, a DC 25 Use Technological Device or Use Psionic Device check is required.

Gruesome Display (Ex): Any ally of the victim within 30 feet who witnesses a Rib Extraction must succeed on a DC 16 Will save or be shaken for 1d4 rounds.
Skills: *An Erb gains a +4 racial bonus on Hide checks in forested or dimly lit terrain.

Anemic Enzyme (Ex): A Grue’s tail secretes a viscous, pale goo. Any living creature hit by its tail slap must succeed on a DC 21 Fortitude save.

The Pallid Curse: Regardless of the save, the victim's hair falls out and their skin turns a sickly, translucent white.

Erb-Bond (Ex): Grues admire the "stature and intellect" of Erbs. While within 30 feet of an Erb, a Grue gains a +2 morale bonus on attack rolls and Will saves.

New Specialized Maneuver: The Meat-Scrum
When an Erb and a Grue act in concert against a single foe, they can perform the following:
The Anemic Shuck (Ex): If a Grue has successfully hit a target with its Anemic Enzyme tail slap in the previous round, the victim’s flesh becomes pale and doughy. Any Erb attacking that specific victim gains a +4 bonus on the check to confirm a Rib-Lock and deals an additional +2 damage per rib removed during Rib Extraction, as the weakened tissues offer less resistance to the Erb’s horns.
Ecological Note: The "Ball"
In a standard "Rugby" encounter, the Erb and Grue will be focused on a Termite Person (see previous block).
The Ball: Small Monstrous Humanoid; HP 9; AC 15.
The Goal: Moving the "ball" into a designated hollow tree or earthen mound.
Combat Trigger: If the PCs interfere with the "ball," the Erb and Grue immediately shift their focus to the party, often viewing the smallest PC (Halfling, Gnome, or Elf) as a "Replacement Ball" for the next half of the game.

Skills: Climb +11, Hide +14*, Move Silently +14, Spot +9
Feats: Improved Initiative (B), Lightning Reflexes, Weapon Finesse (B)
Environment: Derelict spacecraft, high-tech ruins, metallic vaults
Organization: Solitary or Cluster (2–5)
Challenge Rating: 12
Alignment: Always Neutral
Silvered Hide (Ex): The Deathspinner’s body is a high-tensile silver alloy. Its natural attacks are treated as Silver and Magic for overcoming damage reduction. It possesses DR 10/adamantine.
Monofilament Lair (Ex): The spider strings its lair with strands one molecule thick. These are nearly invisible (Spot DC 30). Any creature moving through a square containing these webs must succeed on a DC 22 Reflex save or be "sliced into french fry-like segments," taking 10d6 slashing damage. Success deals 1d6 damage as the victim stops in time.
Molecular Web-Shot (Ex): Once per day, the spider fires a 15-ft. by 15-ft. net of monofilament webbing (Range 30 ft.). Targets must roll one-half their own Dexterity score or less on a d20 to evade (e.g., a Dex 18 character must roll a 9 or less).
Failure: The victim is entangled and takes 5d6 slashing damage per round.
Escape: A DC 25 Strength or Escape Artist check allows escape, but the act of struggling deals an immediate 10d6 slashing damage to the victim regardless of success.
Evasion (Ex): On a successful Reflex save against an effect that deals half damage, the Deathspinner takes no damage instead.
Skills: *Gains a +8 racial bonus on Hide and Move Silently checks in metallic or high-tech environments.
Harvesting & Salvage
A DC 25 Knowledge (Architecture & Engineering) or Survival check (1 hour) allows a character to recover the following:
1. Monofilament Silk (2d4 strands, 10-ft. each)
Usage: Can be used to craft Monofilament Whips (2d6 slashing, 18-20/x3 crit, ignores 5 points of natural armor) or as traps.
Market Value: 2,500 gp per strand.
2. Silver-Alloy Chassis (50 lbs.)
Usage: Can be smelted to create one suit of Medium Fortification silver plate or two +1 Keen silvered weapons. The alloy has a Hardness of 20.
Market Value: 5,000 gp (raw) / 12,000 gp (ingots).
