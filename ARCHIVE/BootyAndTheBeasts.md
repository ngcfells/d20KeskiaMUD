Combat
Fungus men are cautious, preferring to hide in the damp shadows of their caves. They use improvised stalactite daggers and "shields" harvested from giant mushroom tops.
Spore Cloud (Ex): Once per day as a standard action, a fungus man can emit an opaque cloud of spores in a 15-foot diameter centered on itself. This cloud provides total concealment (50% miss chance) to all creatures within it and lasts for 1d4 rounds. Fungus men typically use this to cover their retreat.
Light Sensitivity (Ex): Fungus men are dazzled in bright sunlight or within the radius of a daylight spell.
Skills: *Fungus men receive a +4 racial bonus on Hide checks when in marshy or cavernous terrain with significant fungal growth.

Camouflage (Ex): Since a Living Hill looks like a normal grassy hill when at rest, it takes a DC 30 Spot check to notice it before it attacks. Anyone camping on the hill is considered to be within the creature's "space."
Silent Swallow (Ex): When the Living Hill feeds on a sleeping or unconscious victim, it opens a sod-lined maw beneath them. The victim must succeed on a DC 29 Reflex save or be swallowed. Because the process is nearly soundless, other sleeping creatures must succeed on a DC 25 Listen check to wake up. Even if awake, companions have only a 1-in-6 chance (roll 1d6, result of 6) to notice the victim's sudden absence unless they are observing them directly.
Swallowed Whole (Ex): A swallowed creature is considered incapacitated by paralytic gastric enzymes (DC 27 Fortitude save or paralyzed). The stomach deals 2d6+12 points of bludgeoning damage and 2d6 points of acid damage per round. If the victim remains inside for 10 rounds, they are completely dissolved. A swallowed creature can cut its way out by using a light slashing or piercing weapon to deal 30 points of damage to the gizzard (AC 14).
Skills: *A Living Hill gains a +20 racial bonus on Hide checks in wilderness settings due to its natural appearance.

New Specialized Maneuver: The Meat-Scrum
When an Erb and a Grue act in concert against a single foe, they can perform the following:
The Anemic Shuck (Ex): If a Grue has successfully hit a target with its Anemic Enzyme tail slap in the previous round, the victim’s flesh becomes pale and doughy. Any Erb attacking that specific victim gains a +4 bonus on the check to confirm a Rib-Lock and deals an additional +2 damage per rib removed during Rib Extraction, as the weakened tissues offer less resistance to the Erb’s horns.
Ecological Note: The "Ball"
In a standard "Rugby" encounter, the Erb and Grue will be focused on a Termite Person (see previous block).
The Ball: Small Monstrous Humanoid; HP 9; AC 15.
The Goal: Moving the "ball" into a designated hollow tree or earthen mound.
Combat Trigger: If the PCs interfere with the "ball," the Erb and Grue immediately shift their focus to the party, often viewing the smallest PC (Halfling, Gnome, or Elf) as a "Replacement Ball" for the next half of the game.

 * Logic for Skeletal Extraction and Power Consumption
 */
function onUse(player, target) {
  const item = this;
  if (item.getMeta('charges') <= 0) {
    player.say("The tentacle is withered and dry; it lacks the power to strike.");
    return;

  if (!target.hasSkeleton) {
    player.say("The wand pulses fruitlessly; your target lacks the necessary structure.");
    return;

  item.setMeta('charges', item.getMeta('charges') - 1);

    else if (target.size === 'large') gain = 2;

      duration: Infinity,

         if (!p.checkHalfCon()) {
           p.say("Your chest collapses. You are suffocating.");

         }
      }
    });

Mundane Masking (Ex): The pouch is shielded by a CL 30 Nondetection and Magic Aura effect. It detects as a completely non-magical, mundane cloth sack to all spells, including Detect Magic, Identify, True Seeing, and Analyze Device.
Dimensional Interior (Ex): The interior of the pouch is a static 20-ft. x 20-ft. x 20-ft. pocket dimension.
Encumbrance: Items stored within have no weight for the bearer.
Environment: The space has a self-renewing air supply, though time passes normally (items are not in stasis).
Anti-Thievery Gate (Su): When anyone other than the "Keyed" owner reaches into the bag, they see a glamer of a normal pile of gems and coins. If they attempt to grab any item, the dimensional gate snaps shut.
Severing Trap: The thief must succeed on a DC 30 Reflex save.
Failure: The thief’s arm is instantly severed at the point of entry (usually the elbow or shoulder). This deals 1d6 Strength damage and 4d6 slashing damage, plus heavy bleeding (1d6/round). The severed limb remains inside the pocket dimension.
Success: The thief manages to pull their hand back just as the fabric of space cinches shut, but they retrieve nothing.
Ownership Keying: The pouch is keyed to its current owner's soul. If the owner is sleeping, charmed, or on another plane (Astral/Ethereal), the pouch remains keyed to them. Only upon the permanent death of the owner does the pouch become "unkeyed." The first individual to touch the sack after the owner's death becomes the new "Keyed" owner.

/**
 * Logic for the Severing Gate
 */
function onGetItem(player, item, container) {
  const owner = container.getMeta('keyed_owner');
  
  if (player.name !== owner) {
    player.say("As you reach for the gold, the mouth of the bag cinches tight with impossible force!");
    
    const saved = player.save('reflex', 30);
    if (!saved) {
      player.say("PAIN flares as the bag severs your arm cleanly at the elbow!");
      player.takeDamage(50, 'slashing');
      player.drainAttribute('strength', 4);
      player.addEffect('bleeding');
      return false; // Action failed
    } else {
      player.say("You pull back just in time, the bag snapping shut inches from your fingers.");
      return false;
    }
  }
  return true; // Owner can take items freely
}
